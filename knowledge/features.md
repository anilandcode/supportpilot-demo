# Product Features

## Issues

Issues are the core unit of work in AcmeDesk. Each issue has a title, rich-text description, priority, status, assignee, label, due date, and estimate. Issues can be linked to projects, cycles, and roadmaps. Users can create issues from the keyboard shortcut C, from Slack, or by converting a GitHub comment.

## Projects

Projects group related issues around a goal or deliverable. Each project has a lead, target date, status, and progress indicator calculated from linked issues. Projects can be viewed as a list, board, or timeline. Project leads can publish short status updates for stakeholders.

## Cycles

Cycles are time-boxed iterations, usually one or two weeks, that help teams focus on a defined batch of work. At the end of a cycle, incomplete issues can automatically roll into the next cycle or move back to the backlog. Cycle analytics show completion rate, velocity, and scope creep.

## Roadmaps

Roadmaps show projects and milestones on a leadership-friendly timeline. Roadmaps update automatically when project target dates change and can be shared externally through a read-only link. Roadmaps are available on Pro and above.

## Views and notifications

AcmeDesk supports filters by assignee, label, priority, status, project, cycle, team, creator, and custom fields. Saved views remember filter configurations. The Inbox collects assignments, mentions, comments, and status changes, with email and mobile notification preferences.
